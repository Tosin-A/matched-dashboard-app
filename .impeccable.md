## Design Context

### Users
UK-based matched bettors — financially motivated, detail-oriented, 18–35. Using this tool actively while placing bets: scanning odds, running calculations, tracking offers. Sessions are focused and intentional. Evening/weekend use alongside bookmaker sites.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Profitable. Confident.**
The tool should feel like it already knows you're going to make money. Not flashy — surgical. Every number on screen is money in or money out.

### Aesthetic Direction
Trading 212 / fintech dark. Near-black blue-tinted surfaces, electric blue accent, clean white type. Palette: black, blue, white. Green for profit, red for loss. Dark because traders work in the evening and financial data pops on dark surfaces.

Anti-references: gamer/streamer aesthetics (neon, glow), "startup dashboard" (pastel cards, emoji-heavy), productivity tool energy (lots of rounded corners, whitespace-heavy).

Theme: **Dark**
Fonts: Barlow Semi Condensed (numbers + headings) + Hanken Grotesk (body/UI)
Accent: electric blue `oklch(0.57 0.215 253)`
Profit: money green `oklch(0.64 0.160 145)`

### Design Principles
1. **Numbers are the hero** — financial figures are crisp, condensed, instantly scannable
2. **Dark with conviction** — not brooding, not edgy — purposeful dark for a professional financial tool
3. **Signal over noise** — if it doesn't tell you what to bet or what you've made, it should be invisible
4. **Status is always visible** — profit/loss in the header at all times
5. **Typography does the work** — hierarchy through weight and size, not cards and decoration
